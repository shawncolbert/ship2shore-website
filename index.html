How a vehicle actually gets released

The four-link clearance chain.

Every vehicle sitting at the port is held up by one of these four links. We check all four directly with the port — not the broker — before anything gets scheduled.

01
Steamship Line

Freight hold check

We confirm there's no unpaid balance to the shipping line — the most common reason a vehicle sits without anyone noticing.

02
Customs

Inspection & stamp

We verify customs has scheduled — and completed — its inspection, and that the clearance stamp has actually been issued.

03
Bill of Lading

Original B/L on file

Customs can't even schedule an inspection until the original Bill of Lading has been submitted. We confirm it's in.

✓
Gate Pass

Cleared for release

Once Ports America issues the gate pass, your vehicle is cleared — no clearance call needed, no waiting on a broker's guess.

i

We never schedule a pickup before all four links confirm clear. If something's holding your vehicle, we tell you exactly which link it is and what needs to happen — straight from the port's own system, not a broker's assumption.

A Common Source of Confusion

The port has the final say. The broker doesn't.

Brokers often tell clients a vehicle is "cleared" when it isn't — not out of dishonesty, but because they simply can't see what the port can see.

What a broker knows

  • Only what the steamship line happens to tell them
  • May guess or assume clearance status
  • No direct visibility into port-level holds or fees
VS

What the port knows

  • Actual vessel clearance status, straight from their system
  • Actual customs clearance status, confirmed directly
  • Final say on every release — no exceptions, no guessing
The One Field That Stops Everything

Wrong name on the delivery order? The port won't release your car.

Almost every delivery order arrives with the wrong name in the "Local Delivery or Transfer By" field — a broker, the customer's own name, "Self Pickup," or it's just left blank.

Before any vehicle can come out of the gate, that field has to read Ship2Shore Booking. As your consignee, we correct it the moment you hire us — no extra step on your end.
